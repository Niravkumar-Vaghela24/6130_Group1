# ALY 6130 – Risk Management for Analytics
## Group 1 | Manulife Financial × Mahindra Life Insurance JV — ERM Analysis

This repository contains the Enterprise Risk Management analysis for **Manulife Financial Corporation's strategic 50:50 life insurance joint venture with Mahindra & Mahindra in India** — announced November 2025, with the CEO appointed February 2026. The analysis was conducted through a comprehensive SWOT and ERM framework focusing on real, current business developments.

---

## 🏢 About the Company

**Manulife Financial Corporation** (TSX/NYSE: MFC) is a leading international financial services company headquartered in Toronto, Canada. Founded in 1887, Manulife serves 36M+ customers across 15+ countries and manages CAD $1.6 trillion in assets under management. Their primary growth engine is Asia-Pacific, where they hold leading insurance positions across Hong Kong, Singapore, Vietnam, Philippines, and Indonesia.

---

## 🤝 The Joint Venture — Why India?

Manulife is entering India's life insurance market through a **50:50 JV with Mahindra & Mahindra** — one of India's most trusted conglomerates known for tractors, SUVs, and rural financial services through Mahindra Finance.

**Why this makes strategic sense:**
- India has **1.4 billion people** but only **2.7% insurance penetration** — one of the lowest globally
- The market is projected to reach **USD $244.5 billion by 2031** growing at 9.4% CAGR
- India's government has set a national target of **"Insurance for All by 2047"**
- Mahindra Finance already serves **400,000+ villages** through its rural lending network — giving the JV instant last-mile distribution that no foreign insurer can replicate alone
- Both companies already have a **proven 5-year partnership** through Mahindra Manulife Investment Management (launched 2020)

## ⚖️ SWOT Analysis

The SWOT was conducted through an ERM lens specifically around the JV acquisition scenario.

| | Internal | External |
|---|---|---|
| **Positive** | Existing partnership eliminates cold-start risk; Manulife's global AI underwriting expertise; Mahindra's unmatched rural distribution | India's massively underpenetrated market; Bima Sugam digital marketplace; composite insurance license opportunity |
| **Negative** | No prior IRDAI regulatory experience; legacy system integration complexity; large sensitive policyholder data exposure | LIC's 57%+ market dominance; rural digital infrastructure gaps; advanced cyber threats under India's DPDPA |
